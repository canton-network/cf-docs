---
title: "How to Propose a CIP"
description: "Step-by-step process for drafting and submitting a Canton Improvement Proposal through the GSF repository"
---

Before proposing a CIP, make sure you understand what CIPs are and how they fit into Canton Network governance. See [What Are CIPs?](/docs-main/overview/reference/what-are-cips) for background.

## Before You Start

You need two things before submitting a CIP:

- **A GitHub account** with access to the [CIP repository](https://github.com/global-synchronizer-foundation/cips)
- **Super Validator sponsorship** -- at least one SV must sponsor your proposal and one must endorse it to move it to "Proposed" status. If you represent an SV, you only need one additional endorser.

## Step-by-Step Process

### 1. Check for Existing Proposals

Search the [CIP repository](https://github.com/global-synchronizer-foundation/cips) to make sure your idea has not already been proposed. If a related CIP exists, consider whether your idea extends it or conflicts with it. Building on existing proposals is often more productive than starting from scratch.

### 2. Discuss the Idea Informally

Float your idea on the [GSF mailing lists](https://lists.sync.global/) before writing a formal proposal. The `cip-discuss` list is the right venue. This early feedback helps you gauge interest, identify potential objections, and refine the proposal scope. For CIPs that affect tokenomics, the [tokenomics committee list](https://lists.sync.global/g/tokenomics/topics) is also relevant.

Community Slack channels like `#gsf-global-synchronizer-appdev` and `#gsf-outreach` are good places for initial, less formal conversations.

### 3. Write a Draft CIP

Copy the template directory (`cip-XXXX`) from the [CIP repository](https://github.com/global-synchronizer-foundation/cips) and fill in each section. Your CIP must include these sections:

- **Preamble** -- metadata headers including CIP number (use `?` until assigned), title (44 characters max), author, type, status (`Draft`), created date, and license
- **Abstract** -- a concise summary of roughly 200 words
- **Motivation** -- the problem your proposal solves and why the current state is insufficient
- **Specification** -- the precise technical or procedural changes you propose
- **Rationale** -- why you chose this design over alternatives
- **Backwards Compatibility** -- any breaking changes and how they are handled
- **Reference Implementation** -- required for Standards Track CIPs; code or a pointer to a working implementation

The preamble `Type` field must be one of: Standards Track, Governance, Tokenomics, Process, or Informational. Choose the one that best fits your proposal.

For licensing, `CC0-1.0` is the default. `Apache-2.0` is also recommended. The full list of acceptable licenses is in [CIP-0000](https://github.com/global-synchronizer-foundation/cips/blob/main/cip-0000/cip-0000.md).

### 4. Submit a Pull Request

Open a pull request against the [CIP repository](https://github.com/global-synchronizer-foundation/cips) with your draft. A CIP editor will review it for format compliance, assign a CIP number, and update the preamble headers. CIP editors check formatting and structure -- they do not judge the merits of the proposal itself.

The current CIP editors are listed in [CIP-0000](https://github.com/global-synchronizer-foundation/cips/blob/main/cip-0000/cip-0000.md).

### 5. Respond to Review Feedback

Once your PR is open, expect feedback from CIP editors, SV representatives, and community members. Discussion deadlines vary by type:

- Standards Track: 3 months
- Governance, Tokenomics, Process: 1 month
- Informational: 2 weeks

Revise your draft based on the feedback and push updates to the same PR.

### 6. Move Through the Lifecycle

A CIP progresses through these stages:

- **Draft** -- you are writing and refining the proposal
- **Proposed** -- the CIP has SV sponsorship and is submitted to the `cip-vote` mailing list for a formal vote
- **Approved** -- the CIP receives a 2/3 majority vote from Super Validator representatives (voting runs for 10 days)
- **Active / Final** -- non-on-chain changes become Active immediately; on-chain changes become Final when 2/3 of SVs have adopted them

A CIP can also end up as Withdrawn, Rejected, Replaced, or Obsolete.

## Review Bodies

Different CIP types go through different reviewers:

- **Tokenomics CIPs** are reviewed by the [tokenomics committee](https://lists.sync.global/g/tokenomics/topics), which evaluates economic impact and incentive alignment.
- **Standards Track CIPs** are reviewed by technical experts within the SV community and the development teams.
- **Governance and Process CIPs** are discussed broadly among SVs on the mailing lists and in operator channels.

## After Approval

An approved CIP is not automatically enforced. For on-chain changes, SV operators coordinate timing on the `supervalidator-ops` mailing list and then create on-chain vote proposals that include an expiration date, an effectivity date, and a link to the approved CIP pull request. The on-chain vote follows the same [BFT governance mechanism](/docs-main/overview/reference/sv-governance-reference) used for all DSO actions.

Hard-fork CIPs require adoption across the entire community -- both Super Validators and validators must upgrade.
